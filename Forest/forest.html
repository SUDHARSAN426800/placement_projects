Tiger

Tigers are large, powerful, striped felines, known for their strength and hunting prowess.
They are the largest members of the cat family and are known for their orange coat with black stripes.
Tigers are apex predators, playing a crucial role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems.


Birds

Birds are warm-blooded, feathered vertebrates that typically fly using wings and lay hard-shelled eggs.
They are incredibly diverse, with over 18,000 species found all over the world.
Birds play vital roles in ecosystems, and many have unique adaptations, such as specialized beaks for feeding or vibrant plumage for attracting mates.


Snake

Snakes are legless reptiles with long, slender bodies covered in scales.
They are carnivores, meaning they eat meat, and some are venomous,
using their fangs to inject toxins into their prey or for defense.
Snakes can be found in various habitats worldwide, excluding polar regions.
They are known for their forked tongues, which they use to "smell" their surroundings.
